# The Ops Engine — Brand & Content Standard

_Provisional name (studio/system direction). Owner confirms final name + handle availability. One-line config change in `.env`._

## Positioning
**The Ops Engine** = faceless studio that shows **service-business owners** the automations that quietly run their business in the background — capturing leads, calls, bookings, and reviews they're currently losing.

- **Audience (content):** owners/operators of local service businesses (trades, clinics, salons, gyms, cleaners, agencies).
- **Beachhead (service outreach):** horizontal — test across several local verticals week 1, double down on whoever bites.
- **Differentiator:** real, technical, outcome-driven builds (not "get rich with AI" hype). We sell recovered revenue, not dreams.
- **Tone:** blunt, concrete, money-focused. Numbers over adjectives. No hype, no "in this video."

## Content pillars
1. **Money leaks** — what you're losing (missed calls, slow lead response, no-shows, lost reviews).
2. **The fix** — one specific automation, explained simply, with the outcome.
3. **Tool spotlights** — AI/automation tools an owner can use (affiliate-eligible).
4. **Proof / mini-case** — "this business recovered $X" (real once we have clients).

## Scripting standard (matches titan `parse_reel_script`)
- 30–40s, 4–6 scenes. **Hook in first 1.5s** = a dollar amount or a loss.
- One idea per video. Spoken line ≈ 1 sentence. Caption = 3–6 word punch in CAPS.
- Every video ends with a **CTA to the lead magnet** (free template/audit → email). Monetization on from video #1.
- b-roll keywords must be Pexels-findable, vertical-friendly (people, phones, work sites, screens).

Format per script:
```
### Script N: "Title"
**Product:** TheOpsEngine
**Hook (0-1.5s):** ...
**Scene 1:** spoken | caption: CAPS PUNCH | broll: search keyword
**CTA:** ... link in bio.
```
